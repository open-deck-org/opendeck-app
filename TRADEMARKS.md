# Trademark & Fork Policy

The OpenDeck **code** is open source under the Apache License 2.0. The OpenDeck
**name, logo, and icon** are not — they are trademarks of Sinisha Djukic. This
is the normal split for an open project with an official store presence, and
this document spells out exactly what that means for you, so there are no
surprises.

## The short version

- **The code is yours to use.** Apache 2.0 lets you use, modify, distribute, and
  build commercial products on this code, for free, forever. Nothing here takes
  that away.
- **You can ship your own viewer.** Fork it, build it, publish it to any store
  or the web. We *want* alternative players for the `.deck` format.
- **Just don't ship it as "OpenDeck."** A fork distributed to end users —
  especially in an app store — must use its own name and its own icon, and must
  not present itself as the official OpenDeck app.

## What's covered

The trademarks include, without limitation: the name **"OpenDeck"**, the
OpenDeck logo and app icon, and confusingly similar variants.

## What you may do without permission

- Use the code under Apache 2.0, including in commercial and closed-source
  products.
- State, factually and accurately, that your product is "built on OpenDeck,"
  "a fork of OpenDeck," or "compatible with the OpenDeck `.deck` format." Nominal,
  truthful references like these are fine.
- Implement the `.deck` format freely — it is a separate, CC0 specification with
  no trademark strings attached.

## What requires a different name and icon

If you distribute a modified version to end users — particularly through an app
store — you **must**:

1. Choose a distinct product name that is not "OpenDeck" and is not confusingly
   similar.
2. Replace the OpenDeck logo and app icon with your own.
3. Not imply that your build is the official OpenDeck app or is endorsed by the
   OpenDeck project.

This protects users (one "OpenDeck" in a store means a predictable, signed
build) and it keeps the playing field level: the official build has no code
advantage over yours — the only thing reserved is the name.

## The official builds

The official OpenDeck apps in the app stores are built, signed, and published by
Sinisha Djukic. The app is, and will remain, **free** — no paid tiers, no
in-app purchases. Source for the official build is this repository; the only
things not in the repository are the signing keys, store credentials, and
branded assets.

## Questions

If you're unsure whether a use is okay, or you'd like permission for something
this policy doesn't cover, open an issue or reach out before shipping. We're
happy to say yes to reasonable things.

*This policy may be updated over time; updates won't retroactively restrict the
Apache 2.0 grant on any code you've already received.*
